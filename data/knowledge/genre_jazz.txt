Jazz Music
Jazz is built on improvisation, complex harmonies, and rhythmic freedom. Core elements:
- Syncopated, swinging rhythm patterns
- Sophisticated chord progressions (often with chromatic movement)
- Improvisational solos by instrumentalists
- Common instruments: piano, upright bass, drums, saxophones, trumpets
- Relaxed, often mid-to-low energy (though upbeat jazz exists)
- Emphasis on musician conversation and interaction

Jazz is cerebral and emotional, rewarding close listening. Its conversational nature between instruments creates intimacy and sophistication.
Moods range from sad and introspective (melancholic ballads) to joyful and celebratory (bebop, swing).
Jazz influenced countless genres and remains a gold standard for musical sophistication and emotional depth.
